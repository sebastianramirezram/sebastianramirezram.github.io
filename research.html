Working Paper Behavioral Econ Social Norms Experiments

The Tired and the Blind: The Effect of Cognitive Load on Social Norms

Aycinena, D., Lopez, S., & Ramírez, S.

Examines how cognitive load and mental fatigue alter individuals' adherence to and perception of social norms. Using controlled laboratory experiments, the paper investigates the causal pathway between limited cognitive bandwidth and norm-following behavior, with implications for mechanism design in resource-constrained environments.

Working Paper Labor Economics Crime Youth Policy

Nothing Stops a Bullet like a Job? The Long-Term Impact of Summer Youth Employment Programs on Violent Crime

Modestino, A., Golestani, A., Turchan, B., Struhl, B., Braga, A., & Ramírez, S. (2025)

Investigates the causal long-run effects of summer youth employment programs on violent crime outcomes. The paper leverages quasi-experimental variation to estimate whether short-term employment opportunities during adolescence produce durable reductions in violent crime, contributing evidence to the intersection of labor market policy and public safety.
